Hello World!

18 May 2023 — Hello

Hi! I’m Zachary Lihn, an undergraduate math major at Columbia University, and this is my first post on this personal website.

I’m hoping to use this website as a personal website and blog. I expect that the content will largely be math-focused, and I’m hoping to use this space to upload notes, expository articles, and research updates.

To that end, here’s a quick summary of where my life is right now and what I want to do for the next few months.

  • I just finished my fourth semester at Columbia, and I finished the Algebraic Geometry and Modern Geometry (i.e. Riemannian geometry) sequences. Algebraic Geometry was a standard introduction to varieties/schemes course. Modern Geometry did most of Do Carmo’s book, and finished with connections of principal bundles. We also started Witten’s proof of the positive energy theorem but didn’t get too far due to the semester ending.
  • Tomorrow, I’ll be travelling to Paris, where I’ll be at the Université Paris Diderot learning about the Geometry and Dynamics of Moduli Spaces. The plan is to blog about this topic while I’m there.
  • When I get back, I’ll be at Columbia learning about Complex Geometry and Kahler manifolds. I plan on blogging about my reading and doing a long series on complex geometry.
  • I’ll also be a Teaching Assistant for Analysis II over the summer. I’m required to attend the lectures, so I might take notes and post them here if I have time.
  • Finally, at some point I want to finish the proof of the Positive Energy Theorem. I also want to write a little bit about Seiberg-Witten theory if I have time.

Hopefully, putting these plans on paper will make me more motivated to post to this blog. See you soon!
